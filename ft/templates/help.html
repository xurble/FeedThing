Help

FeedThing is a personal, web-based feed reader with an emphasis on simplicity and preventing information overload.

FeedThing can track the posts you have read in each feed and make sure you never miss a post, or it can act as a River of News aggregor. Or you can mix the two.

Reading Feeds

For tracked feeds, FeedThing does things a bit differently than most 3-pane agregators in an effort to make catching-up with feeds natural and low-friction. Specifically:

  • There are only two panes - clicking on an unread feed loads all unread posts.
  • Unread posts are presented in chronological order so you can catch-up by reading downwards like a human being.
  • Reading a feed automatically marks all posts as read so there so it's low effort to declare unread-post bankruptcy and bring yourself up to date.

Adding Feeds

You can add feeds via the "Add Feed" menu item or with this bookmarklet.

Simply drag this button onto your bookmark bar: Subscribe in FeedThing

Find out more about bookmarklets

If you want to import your subscription list from another service, you can do that in Add Feed page as well, if you have your feeds in OPML format.

Organising Feeds

From the "Manage Feeds" menu item, you can see details about all your subscriptions. Feeds can be put into Folders e.g. by topic and both Feeds and Folders can be set to display as a River.

There is (deliberately) only one level of hierarchy. No folders inside folders.

As an example I have my FeedThing set up as follows:

  • Two River Folders - Fast Feeds (containing the feeds so some very prolific bloggers that such as kottke.org and waxy.org that I will never keep up with) and News (containing, well, news).
  • All my other feeds at the root level with the exception of one or two authors that write in multiple places. They get a folder each to group them, but not in river mode. I still read every post.

By default feeds will get the clever-clever name that is published in their RSS. You can override this to remind you what an infrequently-updated feed actually is in the Manage Feeds page and also by clicking the settings button at the top of reading pane when you load a feed.
